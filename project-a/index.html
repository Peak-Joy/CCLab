Family:

The Arachnid Teuthida belongs to the genus of spiders and is the only spider that has evolved to live underwater.

Habitat:

It lives in bioluminescent underwater caves and camouflages itself by changing colors to blend in with its surroundings.

Appearance:

It's main body is circular and it has a gelatinous outer shell that slowly moves with the waves. It has 4 distinct legs extending towards the floor and it changes colors when disturbed.

What we know so far about Arachnid Teuthida? Discovered in the deepest parts of the Atlantic ocean, the Arachnoteuthis is a hybrid between a spider and an octopus that feeds off of smaller fish by absorbing the nutrients in it's outer shell. It lives in bioluminescent underwater caves and camouflages itself by changing colors to blend in with its surroundings. The creature uses its tentacles to attach itself to the cave floor and crawl around like a spider as its web sack makes it too buoyant to swim effectively. It is aggressive but not lethal to humans as it has no venom and it is too small to devour humans, but being too curious can lead to missing fingers.
Guidelines for human interaction with this creature This creature will crawl around the floor chasing you and if you get too far it will stretch to still follow you.
