What we know so far about Arachnid Teuthida? Discovered in the deepest parts of the Atlantic ocean, the Arachnoteuthis is a hybrid between a spider and an octopus that feeds off of smaller fish caught by its web. It lives in bioluminescent underwater caves and camouflages itself by changing colors to blend in with its surroundings. When small fish enter the cave it shoots a packet of webs that expand after contact, trapping the fish to consume. The creature uses its tentacles to attach itself to the cave walls and crawl around like a spider as its web sack makes it too buoyant to swim effectively. It is aggressive but not lethal to humans as it has no venom and it is too small to devour humans, but being too curious can lead to missing fingers. It will shoot its webs at humans and chase them around.
